Our History

TechCon began in 2010 as a small community event for local developers and entrepreneurs eager to share their passion for technology. What started with just a few dozen attendees quickly gained momentum, attracting innovators from around the globe.

Mission

TechCon’s mission is to empower innovators, creators, and leaders to shape the future of technology. We believe bold ideas and collaboration can solve real-world challenges and improve lives everywhere. Through inspiring talks, hands-on workshops, and powerful networking, we aim to spark ideas, fuel collaboration, and drive progress in the tech community. At its heart, TechCon is more than a conference—it’s a movement to inspire the next generation of technology.

Notable Past Speakers

Genevieve Bell

Genevieve Bell is an anthropologist, author, and distinguished technologist known for exploring the intersection of culture, technology, and human behavior. She has given multiple TED and TEDx talks on ethical AI, the value of boredom in a digital world, and what it means to be human in a rapidly automated society. Her research has influenced how large technology companies think about human-centered design, and her works have shaped policies for responsible computing and emerging technologies.

Ursula Burns

Ursula Burns made history as the first African-American woman to lead a Fortune 500 company (Xerox), and the first woman to succeed another woman in that role. She led Xerox through significant transformation, championed STEM education and broad inclusion, and has been an inspiration and mentor to many in tech leadership. Her speeches often combine business leadership & transformation with social impact.
